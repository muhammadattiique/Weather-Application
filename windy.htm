Why is it so windy?

ByElizabeth Rizzini
Lead Weather Presenter
  • Published
    18 December 2024
Updated 19 December 2024

Thursday will be another blustery day in the UK but the change of wind direction means it will feel much colder.

Strong winds affected much of the country on Wednesday but particularly across northern England and south-west Scotland with gusts of wind of 50-60mph (80-96 km/h).

They were caused by an area of low pressure containing the remnants of a storm – Dorothea - which hit the Canary Islands over the weekend.

Dorothea brought gusts of wind of more than 62mph (100km/h) and left 9,000 households in Tenerife without power.

Cold wind developing

It is going to stay windy even after the remains of Storm Dorothea clear overnight on Wednesday as there is another deep area of low pressure heading our way.

The winds will strengthen again on Thursday but instead of driving in mild air from the south-west it will feel very different. It will be colder with that Arctic flow but still very windy.

There are Met Office weather warnings in force for strong winds on Saturday for north-west Scotland and Northern Ireland where gusts could reach 80mph (129km/h).

A strengthening jet stream

Low pressure systems which bring us these strong winds are being steered towards us by a strengthening jet stream.

The jet stream is the ribbon of fast-moving air high in the atmosphere that pushes a lot of our weather towards us from the Atlantic.

It is not unusual to see a strong jet stream at this time of year but this very windy spell comes soon after Storms Bert and Darragh, which brought exceptionally widespread and damaging strong winds.

When will it be calmer?

Following the brisk and cold north-westerlies at the weekend it looks calmer and milder again by Christmas Eve.
